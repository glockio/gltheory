## PART 3: E=mc² SECTION REVIEW

**Line 235 has a physics concern:**

> "So we get sum(edges) * c, now the graph is 3D, so its c*2"

This is a bit hand-wavy and the "3D so c*2" reasoning doesn't quite track. The c² comes from dimensional analysis (energy = kg·m²/s², mass = kg, so the conversion factor must be m²/s² = velocity²), not from 3D-ness. The existing physics card on "Why c Is Squared" (lines 249–251) already explains this correctly.

**Recommendation:** Simplify the main text to say that E=m in natural units and c² is the unit conversion, then let the physics card do the heavy lifting. Something like:

> "Mass is the sum of all those self-perpetuating operations. Energy is the same thing, freed from the topology. They're the same. E = mc² is the conversion factor between how we measure mass (kilograms) and how we measure energy (joules). The c² is just unit conversion — like inches to centimeters. It's squared because of how the units work out (the physics card below has the details)."

This is more honest and avoids the "3D so c²" claim which doesn't hold up.
