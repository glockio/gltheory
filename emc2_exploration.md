## 1. Dimensional Analysis: The Trivial and Non-Trivial Parts

### Natural Units Setup
- c = 1 hop/tick (dimensionless in graph, but carries dimensions [length]/[time])
- ℓ_P = 1 (fundamental length scale of graph)
- t_P = 1 (fundamental time scale of graph)
- Mass m = number of internal edges in stable topology (dimensionless count)

### The Trivial Part: Why E=m in Natural Units

In natural units where c=1, ℓ_P=1, t_P=1:
- Energy dimensions: [energy] = [ML²T⁻²]
- Mass dimensions: [mass] = [M]
- To make E dimensionless requires: E/m = (energy/mass) has units [L²T⁻²] = c²
- Setting c=1 makes [L²T⁻²]=1, so E=m appears trivial

**Critical insight**: This is purely dimensional sleight-of-hand. The statement "E=m in natural units" doesn't explain WHY the proportionality constant is c², it just hides it by setting c=1.

### The Real Question: Why c² Geometrically?

To make progress, we must ask: **In the graph picture, where do the physical dimensions come from?**

If:
- Each hop is ℓ_P in length
- Each tick is t_P in time
- Edges carry "stuff" that propagates at c = ℓ_P/t_P

Then:
- Mass (edge count) has dimensions [dimensionless count]
- To get energy, we must convert this count into action dimensions [ML²T⁻²]
- The conversion factor must involve t_P and ℓ_P

**Result**: Energy ~ (edge count) × (ℓ_P² / t_P²) = m × c²

This is NOT a graph discovery—it's a consequence of embedding the graph in a space with dimensional structure. The c² emerges because energy is a momentum-squared quantity: E ~ p²/m = (mc)²/m = mc².

## 3. Counting Argument: Edges, Propagation, and Energy

### Setup: Unraveling a Stable Topology

Let the mass topology be a connected set of M edges that self-reproduce each tick, maintaining its shape.

When this topology is "released" (unravels):
- Each of the M edges becomes a free propagating signal
- Each signal carries information/energy
- Each propagates at c = 1 hop/tick

### Question: Does Counting Give mc²?

**Naive counting**:
- M edges, each propagating at speed c
- Total energy = M × (energy per edge) = ?

**Problem**: What is "energy per edge"?

If we say "each edge carries kinetic energy ½c² per unit length," then:
- Total energy = M × ½c² (if distributed uniformly)
- This gives energy ∝ Mc, not Mc²

**Where the c² must come from**:

The kinetic energy of a propagating signal in a relativistic theory is NOT ½c². Instead:
- A mass m traveling at speed c has relativistic energy γmc² where γ = 1/√(1-v²/c²)
- At v → c, γ → ∞, so a massive particle cannot reach c
- But massless signals (photons) have E = pc where p = (energy)/c, giving E ∝ frequency

**In the graph context**: If each edge, when freed, acts like a photon or relativistic particle:
- Its energy is E ~ (information content) × c
- Information content scales as (spatial extent) × c / (time scale) ~ length × bandwidth
- This naturally incorporates c²

**More precisely**:
The energy of a propagating signal is proportional to its "action" S = ∫ L dt (Lagrangian integrated over time).
For a signal traveling distance d at speed c, it takes time t = d/c.
The action dimension requires: S ~ (energy) × (time) = [ML²T⁻¹]
If an edge has "action density" ~ ℓ_P, then releasing M edges gives action ~ M ℓ_P.
To convert action to energy: E = S/t = (M ℓ_P)/(t_P) = M (ℓ_P/t_P) × (ℓ_P) = M c ℓ_P

**But ℓ_P is the fundamental length, and c = ℓ_P/t_P, so**: E = M c² in units where we measure energy properly.

## 5. Connection to Special Relativity: "At Rest" in the Graph

### What Does "At Rest" Mean?

In SR, E=mc² applies to a mass that is at rest in the observer's frame (p=0, v=0).

**In the GL graph, an "at rest" mass topology would be**:
- A stable, closed-form configuration of edges
- One that reproduces itself identically each tick
- Exhibiting no net drift or flow in any spatial direction

**The key property**: Its edges are recirculating internally, not propagating outward. They form a closed loop (topologically) or a tight knot of self-connections.

### Energy Content of a Stationary Topology

For a stationary mass topology:
- The internal edges maintain the shape (binding energy = structure)
- There is no kinetic energy (no bulk motion)
- Total energy = rest mass energy = mc²

**When it unravels** (like nuclear decay or pair annihilation):
- The binding energy is released (internal edges separate)
- Each edge now propagates outward at c (or joins other propagating signals)
- The total energy released = the energy that was "locked up" in the stable topology
- This energy = mc² (from relativistic physics)

### Why the Unraveling Preserves mc²

The principle of energy conservation requires:
- Energy before unraveling = energy after unraveling
- Before: stationary mass topology at rest, energy = mc²
- After: dispersed propagating signals, total energy = ∑ E_i = mc²

This is NOT derived from the graph structure—it's imposed by conservation laws. The graph structure must be compatible with these laws, but doesn't generate them.
