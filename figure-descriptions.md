## Chapter 6: Double Slit

### Figure 12 — Double Slit as Graph Events

> Placement: After the basketball analogy — "This would be like shooting a basketball and finding out it turns into a wave and goes through both nets."

```
@fig:double-slit-graph
Whiteboard style, white background, DM Sans font. Two panels stacked vertically.

TOP PANEL — labeled "NO CONSUMER AT THE SLITS"
Left side: a green "Source" node emits edges toward two slits (drawn as two gaps in a vertical barrier). Because no consumer exists at the slits, the node at each slit is PURPLE and DASHED (pending). Both paths continue forward from both slits as dashed purple edges fanning outward, overlapping and intersecting — they reach the screen on the right. The screen shows a column of nodes, and where overlapping paths arrive at the same screen node, the node is brighter/larger (constructive) or absent (destructive). This creates the interference pattern — alternating bright and dark bands.

Label: "No one asked which slit. Both paths are pending. They arrive together and interfere."

BOTTOM PANEL — labeled "CONSUMER AT THE SLITS (detector added)"
Same layout, but now a small orange "detector" node sits at each slit. This is a consumer. The slit nodes are now SOLID GREEN (committed). Only ONE path continues from the committed slit to the screen — the other path was never taken (the commitment chose one). The screen shows an even spread of dots — no pattern.

Label: "Someone asked which slit. Path commits. One path only. No interference."

Bottom caption: "The detector doesn't disturb the photon. It's a consumer. It forces the event to commit before both paths can arrive."
@/fig
```

### Figure 13 — The Quantum Eraser

> Placement: After "This is probably the strongest argument I can make to show that these events are pending."

```
@fig:quantum-eraser-graph
Whiteboard style, white background, DM Sans font. A graph-flow diagram reading left to right with three stages.

STAGE 1 — "CRYSTAL: TWIN CREATED"
A single source node splits into two edges at a crystal (drawn as a small diamond shape). Two nodes emerge: "ORIGINAL" (goes upward toward the screen) and "TWIN" (goes downward toward detectors). Both are purple/dashed — pending. Label: "Common causal ancestor. Outcomes are correlated from birth."

STAGE 2 — "ORIGINAL HITS SCREEN"
The original node reaches the screen and commits (turns green). It becomes a dot on the screen. But its value depends on the TWIN — they share causal history.

STAGE 3 — "WHAT HAPPENS TO THE TWIN?"
The twin's path splits into two routes:

Route A (top): Twin goes to a detector that READS which-path info. Label: "Consumer reads → twin commits with path info → when you filter by these, no pattern."

Route B (bottom): Twin goes to the ERASER — a device that destroys which-path info before any consumer reads it. The eraser is drawn as a node with "+1" entering and "−1" leaving, netting to zero. Label: "Captured then erased = null operation. No consumer ever actually read it → when you filter by these, pattern reappears."

On the right side, two small screen images:
- Top: filtered by Route A twins → even scatter, no pattern.
- Bottom: filtered by Route B twins → interference bands visible.

Bottom caption: "The dots never move. The eraser doesn't change the past. It just means no consumer ever actually used the which-path information. +1 then −1 = nothing happened. The events stay pending."
```
